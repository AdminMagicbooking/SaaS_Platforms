What changed

Before

  • Excel schedule, re-typed every Monday
  • WhatsApp groups for dispatch and updates
  • Paper RAMS, signed and lost in vans
  • Job references typed twice into Sage
  • Photos emailed back, sorted by hand
  • “Where’s the van?” calls every hour
  • Variations remembered from end-of-day notes
→

After

  • Drag-and-drop weekly scheduler, conflict-checked
  • Job-scoped messaging, real-time via SignalR
  • RAMS signed on the phone before tap-in
  • Sage Project Charges + Records CSV export
  • Photos snapped in app, tagged, auto-uploaded
  • Live van map — click for driver and site
  • Materials & variations captured on site

A day in the life, now

Tracey in the office books a reactive job in under 30 seconds while still on the phone with the client. Scott opens the worker app on his phone, sees today’s jobs, taps Start Route — Google Maps opens to the site. On arrival he taps Arrived; the job flips to InProgress, the office dashboard updates instantly. The RAMS gate blocks his first action until he signs the template on screen. He fits the parts, snaps a delivery-note photo against the materials list, taps out. The timesheet builds itself. End of week, the office exports Sage Project Charges CSV — the manual data entry step is gone.
