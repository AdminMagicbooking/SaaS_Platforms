# Draft

Most construction firms type each engineer's hours into Sage twice — once in the timesheet spreadsheet, once in Project Charges. The second pass costs an hour a week, every week, and it introduces errors that nobody catches until the client query lands.

Jobs Tracker replaces the spreadsheet, the standalone fleet view, the paper RAMS, and the manual Sage entry with one platform. Field engineers tap in on arrival, tap out at the end of the job — travel minutes and on-site minutes flow into a weekly timesheet that drops straight into Sage's Project Charges import. RAMS are signed digitally on the phone before tap-in, blocked at the server until every required template is signed. Photos go to the right job by themselves. Variations are logged on the spot, value and PO number captured before the engineer leaves the site.

The office runs the week from a drag-and-drop scheduler with conflict detection — no double-booking an engineer already on a job or on leave. The live map shows every van, every driver, every site in real time. Customers calling to say nobody turned up can be answered in three seconds.

Currently piloted with a 30-engineer UK contractor. Production cost on the cloud side: about £18 a month.
