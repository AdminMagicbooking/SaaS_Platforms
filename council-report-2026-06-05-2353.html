Play 1 — UK Property Ecosystem: the three blockers

5 advisors · anonymised peer review · chairman synthesis — 2026-06-05
The question: Two founders, bootstrapped, must resolve the 3 blockers gating Play 1 (FindAllProperty → COREPROMA → Jobs Tracker — an aspirational, not-yet-built funnel). B1: FindAllProperty revenue model (free consumer tool, partner-referral revenue, no contract/price yet — build the valuation stack or buy PropertyData?). B2: D-01 Jobs Tracker — module in COREPROMA Pro (A), standalone SaaS (B), or both (C)? One pilot (CBES). B3: D-06 currency — launch-market (A), all GBP (B), or dual-list (C)? What is the right resolution per blocker, and the right order & timing given two-founder bandwidth?

Chairman's verdict

Where the council agrees high confidence

  1. Buy PropertyData — never build the valuation stack. Unanimous. £28–96/mo is noise; the 20–30 dev-weeks are the only scarce asset.
  2. Blocker 1 is the only real blocker — and it's not "build vs buy." It's "will anyone pay for referrals from a free tool with zero traffic?" A sales task, not an engineering task.
  3. Blocker 2 → Option A (module in COREPROMA Pro). You have one customer, not a market. Standalone multi-tenant = premature scaling.
  4. Blocker 3 → Option B (all GBP). UK-only play, clean investor P&L. A 10-minute decision that gates nothing.
  5. The ecosystem is an investor story, not yet a product. No single human walks search→renovate→operate today.

Where the council clashes tension

  • What an LOI must contain. Executor: a light LOI from 15 cold emails is the kill test. Contrarian: a bare LOI is "a polite email" — demand a price AND volume commitment. A reviewer pushes further: a zero-traffic tool can't promise volume, so run a concierge test (hand-deliver real leads) before trusting any signature.
  • Multi-tenant now or later. The Expansionist alone wants auth/billing built multi-tenant "from day one." Everyone else calls it the trap — speculative plumbing for a market of one.
  • The intent-dataset upside. The boldest idea (a "UK property operating system" on proprietary intent data) is also the most attacked — assumes traffic that doesn't exist and data rights (GDPR / OGL) you may not have.

Blind spots the peer review caught

  • Founder-market fit as a kill criterion. FindAllProperty is consumer adtech/volume; COREPROMA & Jobs Tracker are B2B seat-sales. Two founders running both may be running two companies. Does Play 1 deserve any bandwidth vs the one product with paying customers?
  • CBES concentration risk. One pilot underwrites two products. What if it churns?
  • The LOI may be unprovable on paper — concierge lead delivery is more honest than a signature.
  • GDPR / data-licensing undercut the intent-data thesis.

The recommendation

Treat this as one real blocker + two ten-minute clean-ups, and run the real one as a sales experiment, not a build:

  1. Currency — today. All GBP for Play 1. Close D-06.
  2. D-01 — this week. Option A: Jobs Tracker = a COREPROMA Pro field module reusing existing auth. Keep CBES on its current build. Defer the standalone decision until 5+ paying firms exist. Hits the 2026-07-23 deadline by shrinking the question.
  3. FindAllProperty — 3-week LOI-or-kill, starting now. Stop writing the PRD. Buy PropertyData (£96/mo, zero dev). Pitch ~15 local agents/brokers; get one on a call; secure a priced LOI with a volume expectation — or run a concierge lead test. No priced commitment in 3–4 weeks ⇒ the consumer-funnel thesis is dead, and you've saved 20+ dev-weeks.
⚠ The uncomfortable consensus: let the B1 result decide whether Play 1 exists at all. If FindAllProperty can't validate, fold your bandwidth into COREPROMA + the Jobs Tracker module — the only place with real revenue.
The one thing to do first

This week, send the one-page referral pitch to 15 UK estate agents/brokers and book one call — before any PRD, any code, any PropertyData integration. That conversation tells you what a lead is worth and whether the entire Play 1 funnel has a foundation.

The five advisors in full

Contrarian Only Blocker 1 is real — everything else is procrastination dressed as strategy

Three blockers, but only Blocker 1 is real. A free consumer tool monetised by referral kickbacks is the most overcrowded, lowest-trust corner of UK proptech — Rightmove, Zoopla and a dozen affiliates own those agent/mortgage relationships. "Free OGL data" is zero moat (free to competitors too). The LOI plan is right but raise the bar: an LOI with a price AND volume commitment, or it's a polite email. Buy PropertyData; building a stack to save £96/mo while unvalidated is insane.

Blocker 2 is a trap disguised as a choice — one pilot, productising n=1 is premature; pick A (module), defer standalone until 5+ paying firms. Blocker 3 is a spreadsheet formatting preference — pick B (all GBP) in ten minutes; treating it as gating is the tell the team is avoiding Blocker 1. Order: LOI-or-kill on FindAllProperty first; everything else waits.

First Principles Two "blockers" are documentation problems; one is a real bet you haven't made

Strip this down. Blocker 3 (currency) is not a blocker — a reporting-layer formatting choice; pick launch-market currency, normalise to GBP in the investor P&L, done in an afternoon. It gates nothing. Blocker 2 is a false trilemma — you have one pilot, choosing a GTM architecture for a market of one; keep CBES on existing .NET 9, sell to two more firms manually, let their buying behaviour tell you A vs B; the deadline is fake.

Blocker 1 isn't build-vs-buy valuation — it's "does anyone pay for referrals from a free tool with zero traffic?" Buy PropertyData (£28/mo is noise), spend 100% of founder energy on one signed LOI. No LOI, no v1.0, no ecosystem. "Play 1" is a story you're telling investors before you've proven a single hand-off has value. You're gated by one unproven revenue thesis, not three blockers.

Expansionist FindAllProperty isn't a referral tool — it's a UK property-intent dataset

Buy PropertyData — don't build the valuation stack. FindAllProperty is a UK property-intent dataset: every valuation search signals who's about to buy/renovate/let. Referral fees are the floor, not the ceiling — once you have intent volume you sell enriched lead packages, then a property-intent API back to the same agents/lenders. Land the LOI but price it as data access, not a finder's fee.

Blocker 2: do BOTH — ship as a COREPROMA Pro module first (fast revenue, CBES validates) but architect auth/billing multi-tenant from day one so the standalone is a config flip, not a rebuild. Blocker 3: all GBP — clean P&L an acquirer can underwrite. Order: PropertyData+LOI first, multi-tenant-arch module second, currency last. The upside nobody's pricing: three products + one identity + a proprietary intent dataset = a UK property operating system.

Outsider Who is the single human walking this funnel? If you can't name them, it's a slide

I don't know your industry, but the story doesn't hold. You call it an ecosystem (search → renovate → operate) but the three products serve totally different people: FindAllProperty is a consumer checking a house; Jobs Tracker is a trades-firm boss with 38 engineers; COREPROMA is construction management. Who is the single human walking this funnel? If you can't name them, the funnel is a slide, not a product.

Blocker 1: a free consumer tool monetised by referral kickbacks is adtech/price-comparison (a millions-of-visits volume game), not SaaS — a different skillset from selling COREPROMA seats; two founders running both sounds like two companies. Order: (1) Blocker 3 first, trivial — all GBP today. (2) Blocker 2 — pick A, the module. (3) Blocker 1 last but reframe: get the signed LOI before any PRD or build-vs-buy. Don't build the bridge between three islands before checking anyone wants to cross.

Executor Stop writing the PRD — Monday, email 15 estate agents

Build the valuation stack and you've spent your only 20–30 dev-weeks on plumbing. Buy PropertyData (£96/mo tier) — £1,152/yr is a rounding error. Blocker 1 first, but it's a SALES task, not a dev task: stop writing the PRD; this week send a one-page email to 15 local estate agents ("free tool sends you qualified vendor leads, pay £X per completion"). You price referrals by getting one agent on a call. Make the ≥1 LOI gate the literal next action. No LOI from 15 emails in 3 weeks ⇒ the thesis is dead and you saved 20 weeks.

Blocker 2: Option A, module in COREPROMA Pro — you have one customer, not a market; reuse existing auth; hit 2026-07-23 by descoping to "module," not "platform." Blocker 3: Option B, all GBP, one afternoon. Order: LOI emails this week, module build after LOI lands, currency rule today.
